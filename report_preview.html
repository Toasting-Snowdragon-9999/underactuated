Abstract. This paper presents a MATLAB implementation of an underactuated cart with a serial double pendulum. The model is derived in Euler-Lagrange form and verified through energy conservation, equilibrium checks, numerical and analytic linearization, and controllability analysis. Two controllers are discussed: a local LQR stabilizer and an energy-shaping swing-up controller that hands off to LQR near upright.

1 Introduction

Underactuated mechanical systems have fewer independent actuators than degrees of freedom. They appear in robotics, aerospace systems, legged locomotion, pendulum benchmarks, and flexible structures. Their behavior is challenging because not every coordinate can be directly commanded; the controller must exploit natural coupling between actuated and unactuated coordinates.

The MATLAB project studies a cart with a serial double pendulum. The cart can move horizontally and is actuated by a single force. The pendulum links are passive, so the controller influences their motion indirectly through cart acceleration. The project develops a nonlinear model, verifies physical consistency, and compares a local LQR balance controller with a nonlinear energy-shaping swing-up controller.

2 System Model

The generalized coordinates are q = [s, theta1, theta2]^T. The angles are measured from the upward vertical, so theta = 0 is upright and theta = pi is hanging. The state vector is x = [s, theta1, theta2, sdot, theta1dot, theta2dot]^T.

Figure 1. Cart with serial double pendulum.

The model in underactuated_model.m is written as M(q)qddot + C(q,qdot)qdot + G(q) = Bu. The input matrix B = [1, 0, 0]^T applies force only to the cart coordinate, so the angular coordinates are passive.

3 Controller Design

The first controller is a local LQR stabilizer. It linearizes the nonlinear model around the upright equilibrium, solves the continuous-time Riccati equation, and applies u = -Kx. This controller is efficient and systematic, but its validity is local.

The second controller is an energy-shaping swing-up controller. It compares the current total energy E with the upright energy Edes, applies a saturated cart force to pump energy, and switches to LQR when both angles and angular velocities are close enough to upright.

4 Simulation Results

The project uses ode45 for numerical integration. simulation.m verifies model behavior through energy conservation, equilibrium checks, linearization, and controllability. controller1_sim.m tests LQR balance from a small upright perturbation. controller2_sim.m tests swing-up from near hanging and plots total mechanical energy against the desired upright energy.

5 Conclusions

The project demonstrates a complete workflow for an underactuated robotics problem: derive a nonlinear model, verify its physical consistency, analyze local linear behavior, and design controllers for different operating regions. The LQR controller is appropriate for local balance, while the energy-shaping controller handles large-angle swing-up before switching to LQR.
