1 Introduction and Problem Statement

Underactuated mechanical systems have fewer independent control inputs than generalized coordinates. This means that some coordinates cannot be commanded directly, even though their motion is still affected by the controlled coordinates through inertial and gravitational coupling.

The MATLAB project studies a cart with a serial double pendulum. The cart moves horizontally and is actuated by a single force u. Two pendulum links are attached in series, and both angular coordinates are passive. The system has three degrees of freedom and one actuator.

q = [s, θ1, θ2]T,   x = [qT, qdotT]T

The control problem is difficult because the upright equilibrium is unstable and cannot be reached by directly actuating the pendulum angles. The cart force must instead create accelerations that transfer energy into the pendulum links.

Figure 1. The cart-double-pendulum system.

2 System Kinematics and Energies

The model assumes a point-mass pendulum structure with massless rods. With angles measured from the upward vertical, the bob positions are derived directly from the link geometry.

r1 = [s + l1 sin θ1,   l1 cos θ1]T
r2 = [s + l1 sin θ1 + l2 sin θ2,   l1 cos θ1 + l2 cos θ2]T

Differentiation gives the velocity expressions used to construct the kinetic energy. The kinetic energy is a quadratic form in qdot, while the potential energy comes from the vertical positions of the masses.

T = 1/2 M sdot² + 1/2 m1(v1·v1) + 1/2 m2(v2·v2)
V = (m1 + m2) g l1 cos θ1 + m2 g l2 cos θ2

The upright configuration has maximum potential energy in this coordinate convention, so swing-up can be described by driving the total energy E = T + V toward the upright reference energy.

Edes = (m1 + m2) g l1 + m2 g l2

3 Euler-Lagrange Derivation

The Lagrangian is L(q,qdot) = T(q,qdot) − V(q). For each generalized coordinate, the Euler-Lagrange equation relates the rate of generalized momentum to the generalized force.

d/dt(∂L/∂qdotᵢ) − ∂L/∂qᵢ = Qᵢ

Only the cart is actuated, so Q = Bu with B = [1, 0, 0]T. Carrying out the derivatives gives the manipulator equation used by the MATLAB model.

M(q)qddot + C(q,qdot)qdot + G(q) = Bu

The mass matrix is obtained from the second partial derivatives of kinetic energy with respect to generalized velocities. The gravity vector is obtained from the derivative of the potential energy. The implemented state derivative solves for qddot at each ODE call.

qddot = M(q)−1(Bu − C(q,qdot)qdot − G(q) − Ffric)
Ffric = [bcart sdot, bpend1 θ1dot, bpend2 θ2dot]T

4 Model Analysis and Verification

The script simulation.m verifies the model before the controllers are evaluated. The first test is energy conservation. In the absence of input and friction, the total mechanical energy should remain constant.

Edot = d/dt(T + V) = qdotT(Bu − Ffric)

When u = 0 and friction is removed, Edot = 0. The script also evaluates the upright and hanging equilibria and expects the nonlinear derivative to be close to zero.

xup = [0,0,0,0,0,0]T,   xdown = [0,π,π,0,0,0]T

Finally, the model is linearized around equilibria. The numerical Jacobian is compared with the analytic linearization from the manipulator form, and controllability is checked from the six-state controllability matrix.

δxdot = Aδx + Bℓδu
C = [Bℓ, ABℓ, A²Bℓ, ..., A⁵Bℓ],   rank(C) = 6

5 Controller Design

control1.m implements a local LQR stabilizer around upright. The nonlinear model is linearized at x* = 0, u* = 0, and the controller minimizes a quadratic cost.

J = ∫(xTQx + uTRu) dt,   uLQR = −Kx

control2.m implements energy-shaping swing-up with cart regulation, angle shaping, damping, saturation, and a hand-off to LQR near upright.

uE = kE(E − Edes) sign(θ1dot cos θ1 + θ2dot cos θ2)
u = uE − ks s − kv sdot − kθ(sin θ1 + α sin θ2) − kd(θ1dot + θ2dot)

control3.m implements a sliding-mode controller on the collocated cart coordinate. It regulates the cart directly and affects the pendulums indirectly through dynamic coupling.

σ = (sdot − sdotdes) + λ(s − sdes),   uSMC = −(Kp s + Kd sdot) − k sat(σ/φ)
